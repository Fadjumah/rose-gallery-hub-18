## What Is Tonsillitis?

Tonsillitis is inflammation of the tonsils‚Äîthe two oval-shaped tissue pads at the back of your throat. Your tonsils are part of your immune system, helping fight infections. When overwhelmed by bacteria or viruses, they become swollen, painful, and sometimes covered with white or yellow patches.

### Types of Tonsillitis:

| Type | Duration | Description |
|------|----------|-------------|
| **Acute Tonsillitis** | 3-14 days | Single episode, usually resolves with treatment |
| **Recurrent Tonsillitis** | Multiple episodes/year | 5-7+ episodes per year requiring evaluation |
| **Chronic Tonsillitis** | Ongoing | Persistent infection lasting months |
| **Peritonsillar Abscess** | Emergency | Pus collection beside the tonsil (Quinsy) |

## Common Causes of Tonsillitis

### Viral Infections (Most Common - 70%)

Most tonsillitis cases in Uganda are caused by viruses:

- **Adenovirus** ‚Äì Common cold virus
- **Epstein-Barr virus** ‚Äì Causes infectious mononucleosis (glandular fever)
- **Influenza virus** ‚Äì Seasonal flu
- **Parainfluenza virus** ‚Äì Respiratory infections
- **Enteroviruses** ‚Äì Various throat infections

### Bacterial Infections (30%)

When bacteria cause tonsillitis, treatment differs significantly:

- **Group A Streptococcus (Strep throat)** ‚Äì Most common bacterial cause
- **Staphylococcus aureus** ‚Äì Can cause severe infections
- **Streptococcus pneumoniae** ‚Äì Especially in immunocompromised patients
- **Haemophilus influenzae** ‚Äì More common in children

**Important:** Bacterial tonsillitis requires antibiotics to prevent complications like rheumatic fever‚Äîa condition still prevalent in Uganda.

## Recognizing Tonsillitis Symptoms

### Primary Symptoms:

**What you'll notice first:**

- Sore throat (often severe)
- Pain when swallowing (odynophagia)
- Red, swollen tonsils
- White or yellow coating on tonsils
- Swollen lymph nodes in the neck
- Fever (mild to high)
- Bad breath (halitosis)

### Symptoms in Children:

Children may show different signs:

- Refusal to eat or drink
- Excessive drooling
- Unusual fussiness or irritability
- Stomach pain or vomiting
- Voice changes or muffled speech
- Difficulty breathing during sleep (snoring)

### Symptoms Suggesting Bacterial Infection:

These signs suggest strep throat requiring antibiotics:

- High fever (above 38.5¬∞C/101¬∞F)
- **No cough** (viral infections usually include cough)
- Swollen, tender neck glands
- White patches or pus on tonsils
- Sudden onset of severe symptoms
- Petechiae (tiny red spots) on the palate

## Home Care for Mild Tonsillitis

### DO: Supportive Measures

**Hydration is key:**
- Drink plenty of warm fluids (tea with honey, warm water)
- Avoid very hot or acidic drinks
- Ice chips or cold water can soothe pain
- Oral rehydration salts if feverish

**Pain and fever relief:**
- Paracetamol (as directed for age/weight)
- Ibuprofen for inflammation (if no contraindications)
- Warm salt water gargles (¬Ω teaspoon salt in warm water)
- Throat lozenges for adults and older children

**Rest and comfort:**
- Complete rest‚Äîtonsillitis is exhausting
- Soft, easy-to-swallow foods (porridge, mashed bananas, soup)
- Humidify the air if possible
- Avoid smoke and dust exposure

## Warning Signs: When to Seek Urgent ENT Care

### Seek Immediate Care If:

**Breathing difficulties:**
- Noisy breathing or stridor
- Difficulty breathing, especially when lying down
- Pauses in breathing during sleep
- Drooling due to inability to swallow

**Severe swallowing problems:**
- Cannot swallow saliva (drooling)
- Cannot drink fluids
- Dehydration signs (dark urine, dry mouth, no tears)

**Signs of abscess (Quinsy):**
- Severe one-sided throat pain
- Voice changes ("hot potato" muffled voice)
- Difficulty opening mouth fully (trismus)
- Neck stiffness or swelling
- High fever not responding to paracetamol

**General danger signs:**
- Neck stiffness with headache (meningitis concern)
- Rash appearing with sore throat
- Symptoms worsening after 3-4 days despite treatment
- Unable to open mouth properly
- Fever lasting more than 5 days

## Tonsillitis Complications in Uganda

### Why Early Treatment Matters:

In Uganda, delayed treatment of strep throat can lead to serious complications:

**Acute Rheumatic Fever:**
- Still common in East Africa
- Affects the heart, joints, and nervous system
- Prevented with proper antibiotic treatment
- Can cause permanent heart valve damage

**Peritonsillar Abscess (Quinsy):**
- Pus collection beside the tonsil
- Requires drainage and hospitalization
- Can obstruct the airway if untreated

**Post-Streptococcal Glomerulonephritis:**
- Kidney inflammation following strep infection
- Causes swelling, blood in urine, high blood pressure

**Other complications:**
- Middle ear infection (otitis media)
- Sinusitis
- Scarlet fever
- Sepsis (blood infection)

## When Is Tonsillectomy Recommended?

### Criteria for Tonsil Removal Surgery:

At Eritage ENT Care in Entebbe, we recommend tonsillectomy for:

**Recurrent tonsillitis:**
- 7+ episodes in one year
- 5+ episodes per year for two consecutive years
- 3+ episodes per year for three consecutive years

**Obstructive symptoms:**
- Sleep apnea due to enlarged tonsils
- Difficulty breathing or eating
- Failure to thrive in children
- Significant snoring affecting sleep quality

**Other indications:**
- Peritonsillar abscess (one or recurrent)
- Chronic tonsillitis not responding to treatment
- Suspected tonsil malignancy
- Asymmetric tonsil enlargement

### What to Expect from Tonsillectomy:

**The procedure:**
- Day surgery or overnight stay
- General anesthesia
- 20-40 minutes operating time
- Recovery: 7-14 days

**Post-operative care:**
- Soft diet for 1-2 weeks
- Pain management is important
- Adequate hydration essential
- Activity restriction for 2 weeks

## Prevention Tips

### Reduce Your Risk of Tonsillitis:

**Hygiene practices:**
- Wash hands frequently with soap
- Avoid sharing eating utensils and cups
- Cover mouth when coughing or sneezing
- Stay away from people with throat infections

- Manage stress levels

**Environmental factors:**
- Avoid cigarette smoke exposure

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is tonsillitis contagious?

**Yes.** Both viral and bacterial tonsillitis spread through:
- Respiratory droplets (coughing, sneezing)
- Direct contact with infected persons
- Sharing food, drinks, or utensils

Keep infected individuals away from others, especially for the first 24-48 hours of treatment.

### Can adults get tonsillitis?

**Absolutely.** While more common in children, adults can get tonsillitis. Adult tonsillitis can be more severe and may indicate underlying health issues requiring evaluation.

### How long does tonsillitis last?

- **Viral tonsillitis:** 7-10 days with supportive care
- **Bacterial tonsillitis:** Improves within 24-48 hours of starting antibiotics
- **Chronic tonsillitis:** May persist for weeks without proper treatment

### Do I need antibiotics for every sore throat?

**No.** Most sore throats are viral and don't need antibiotics. However, bacterial tonsillitis (strep throat) requires antibiotics to prevent complications. An ENT specialist can determine the cause and appropriate treatment.
